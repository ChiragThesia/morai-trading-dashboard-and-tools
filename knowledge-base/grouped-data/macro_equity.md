## Group Summary: Macro & Equity Markets

### Key Insights
- **Macro narratives drive regime changes** more than fundamental valuations—understanding whether the market is currently pricing growth optimism, stagflation fears, Fed policy pivots, or geopolitical risks matters more than absolute P/E ratios for directional trading.
- **Dollar weakness historically correlates with equity strength**, but this relationship breaks down during true risk-off environments when dollar strength is driven by safe-haven flows rather than fundamental rate differentials.
- **Alpha generation increasingly difficult in passive-dominant environment**: The struggle between active stock picking and passive indexing has shifted the expected returns of active strategies lower as more capital migrates to low-cost index funds, requiring higher level of skill to outperform.
- **Market sentiment shifts occur in clusters**: books about market crashes, hedge fund manager positioning changes, and breadth divergences all cluster around market turns, suggesting regime changes are often preceded by detectable behavioral shifts.
- **Earnings season calendars and forward guidance matter as much as quarterly results**: pre-earnings expectations (IV levels, consensus estimates) determine most of the reaction, while actual beats/misses become secondary drivers of post-earnings moves.

### Key Questions
- How do you determine whether a market decline is a buying opportunity (profit-taking in bull market) vs. early stage of a structural bear market (regime shift)?
- What macro indicators do you monitor to predict equity sector rotations (financials leading rallies when rates rise, tech leading in lower-rate environments)?
- How do earnings expectations and surprise factors vary across market cycles, and how should this change your trading approach?
