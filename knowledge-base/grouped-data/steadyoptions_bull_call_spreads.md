## Group Summary: Bull Call Spreads

### Key Insights
- Bull call spreads are directional strategies combining a long call at a lower strike with a short call at a higher strike, providing limited risk and limited profit potential suitable for moderately bullish market views. The cost basis is reduced by selling the higher strike call, improving capital efficiency and ROI compared to simply buying call options outright.
- Maximum profit occurs when the underlying asset closes at or above the short (higher) strike at expiration, calculated as the difference between strike prices minus the net premium paid. Maximum loss is limited to the net premium paid for the spread, making this strategy predictable for position sizing and risk management.
- Breakeven is calculated as the long call strike price plus the net premium paid, establishing a clear threshold where the strategy becomes profitable. The strategy is most effective in moderately bullish markets where price appreciation is expected but not explosive, as the short call caps upside at the higher strike.
- Time decay (theta) becomes increasingly beneficial as expiration approaches, especially if the underlying stays between the two strike prices, making the spread a theta-positive strategy when entered with proper time to expiration (30-60 days optimal).

- What is the optimal time to entry for bull call spreads to maximize theta decay benefit while maintaining adequate time for the position to reach full profit?
- How does implied volatility level impact the attractiveness of bull call spreads versus alternative bullish strategies like directional calls or put selling?
