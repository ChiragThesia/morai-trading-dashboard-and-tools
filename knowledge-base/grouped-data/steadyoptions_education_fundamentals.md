## Group Summary: Education Fundamentals

### Key Insights
- Options trading fundamentals require understanding that puts and calls are tools for different risk exposures: calls give the right to buy (bullish), puts give the right to sell (bearish). Long options have defined, limited risk (premium paid) while short options can have substantial risk without protective collars, making basic position types critical to master before advanced strategies.
- The Greeks (delta, gamma, theta, vega) are essential metrics for understanding options behavior: delta measures directional price sensitivity, gamma measures rate of delta change, theta measures time decay benefit or cost, and vega measures volatility sensitivity. Comprehending these relationships determines whether a trader understands what drives profits and losses in their positions.
- Option payoff diagrams visually show maximum profit, maximum loss, and breakeven points for any strategy, revealing the risk/reward profile at a glance. Spreads (combining multiple options) allow traders to define risk precisely in advance, unlike naked short positions, making spreads fundamental to professional risk management.
- Put-call parity establishes the mathematical relationship between call and put prices for the same strike and expiration, ensuring arbitrage-free pricing. Understanding this principle helps traders identify mispriced options and recognize equivalent risk exposures across different strategy structures.

- How do assignment and early exercise risk differ between different option types, and what practical steps should traders take to manage these risks?
- What does "implied volatility" mean, and why does it matter more than historical volatility for options trading decisions?
