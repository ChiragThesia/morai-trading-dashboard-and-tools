## Group Summary: Earnings Plays Trading Strategies

### Overview
Earnings trading represents one of the most systematic and lucrative event-driven trading regimes in options markets. Unlike traditional directional betting, professional traders approach earnings through the lens of variance risk premium (VRP)—the mispricing between implied volatility (what the market thinks will happen) and realized volatility (what actually happens). Predicting Alpha documents that this premium can be harvested through multi-leg diversification across 100+ uncorrelated earnings events per quarter, with successful traders executing tight tactical rules around entry/exit timing and position structure selection.

### Key Insights
- **Earnings account for 30-70% of annual stock price movements**, concentrating 12-15% of annual volatility into a single catalyst event. This creates a massive supply/demand imbalance: funds and retail traders flood in to buy hedges, while few sell options (Predicting Alpha). The variance risk premium reflects the insurance premium buyers are willing to pay for protection they may never use.
- **Short straddles are the optimal position structure for earnings neutrality**: selling at-the-money straddles on the closest expiration (1-2 DTE) isolates exposure to the earnings event only, capturing IV crush post-event. Strangles provide slightly wider breakevens but dilute the feedback signal; ATM straddles show immediate if your view (IV > realized move) is working. The core trade: sell straddle before close, exit 5-20 minutes after market open.
- **Implied move vs. realized move is the critical metric**: implied volatility pricing bakes in a specific expected move (e.g., FITB: 4.5% implied, 2.8% historical average). Trading the straddle when IV > realized is a volatility arbitrage, not directional betting. The beauty: you don't need directional accuracy, only accuracy on volatility magnitude.
- **Diversification across uncorrelated events is the true edge**: trading 100+ tickers (FITB, COIN, WFC across earnings seasons) means a single $10,000 loss on TSLA doesn't dominate a portfolio of 0.5% position sizes. The insurance analogy (Predicting Alpha): car insurance profits not from predicting individual crashes, but from pooling millions of uncorrelated risks. One big mover doesn't destroy the strategy.
- **IV crush post-earnings is predictable and quantifiable**: implied volatility typically collapses 20-50% in the 5-10 minutes after earnings release, independent of move direction. SteadyOptions notes this comes from both delta unwinding and vega crush as the event uncertainty is resolved. Traders don't rely on the price move; they rely on volatility compressing whether the move was small or large.
- **Calendar spreads (pre-earnings long, sell close to event) offer defined risk with asymmetric payoff**: buying further-dated vol and selling near-term captures the volatility premium expansion into earnings, then benefits from vol crush afterward. Risk is defined; payoff is uncapped (relative to single-leg short premium strategies).
- **Execution rules matter more than analysis**: enter at close (before all news), exit 5-10 min after open (once reality is digested), get good fills (bid/ask spreads widen post-announcement, wait 1-2 min for tightening), don't hold for IV crush to bottom (close when move is clear, let vega erosion take profits).

### Key Questions
- How does the variance risk premium size change as a function of historical volatility, IV percentile, and time-to-event? Can you systematically size positions based on premium magnitude?
- What's the optimal diversification level (10 trades vs. 50 vs. 100) and position sizing to maximize risk-adjusted returns while controlling for liquidity in closing trades?
- How do implied move estimates vary across platforms, and which are most reliable for predicting realized moves on specific names?

### Major Patterns & Themes
- **IV compression into earnings is systematic and predicts volatility regime changes**: The term structure flattens into earnings (4-10% IV premium in expiration month vs. adjacent months), then steepens after. Traders who understand this 5-point IV differential can size position vega appropriately.
- **A two-stage earnings strategy exists**: (1) Pre-earnings (3-7 days out): capture IV expansion via calendar spreads or ratio spreads; (2) Event earnings (day-before to day-after): short straddles/strangles to capture IV crush. Some professionals run both legs simultaneously on different underlyings.
- **Non-directional structure is non-negotiable for retail traders**: buying protective wings (straddle with long OTM calls/puts) seems prudent but eats 30-50% of premium collected, making the expected value math break. Professional risk management comes from position sizing (0.5% per trade) and diversification, not hedging.
