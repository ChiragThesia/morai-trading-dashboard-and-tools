<objective>
Close WR-02 and WR-04 — two accuracy/safety hazards in the `interpolateRiskReversal` domain
function. WR-04 (W3): `usablePoints` applies no delta-sanity filter, so a mis-signed or numerically
unstable BSM delta (e.g. a deep-ITM solve) could land in the wrong wing (puts use `delta < 0`,
calls `delta > 0`) and distort the ±25Δ bracket. WR-02 (W2): `interpAtDelta` brackets the target
with the tightest spanning pair but does not bound the bracket width, so a sparse smile can
interpolate linearly across a very wide non-adjacent gap and report a real number far from the true
25Δ vol.

Purpose: make the RR domain self-protecting (defense-in-depth — the band is enforced upstream in
`fetchChain.isInFilter`, but the analytics layer must not trust it blindly) and make the
bracket-width policy an explicit, tested decision rather than silent behavior.

Output: a one-line `|delta| >= 1` drop in `usablePoints` (W3) and a decided + documented + tested
bracket-width policy (W2), with fast-check property coverage. No use-case or adapter changes — this
is pure domain, isolated to risk-reversal.ts and its tests.
</objective>
