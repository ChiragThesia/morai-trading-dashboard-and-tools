<objective>
Wire settlement-aware fractional time-to-expiry into the web scenario kernel and make the kernel able
to accept per-leg carry, without changing any existing caller's behavior beyond the day-count fix.
Two additive edits: `pair-calendars.ts` gains `dteExact()` (the exact fractional-days twin of the
display `dte()`), and `scenario-engine.ts`'s `AnalyzerPosition` gains optional fractional-DTE and
per-leg carry fields that the three pricing helpers resolve with graceful fallbacks, plus the
365→365.25 day-count fix.

Purpose: this is the consumption half of CONTEXT item 1 (fractional DTE) and the kernel plumbing for
item 2 (carry) — the engine must be able to price each leg with an exact T and its own r/q before
plan 34-05 supplies those values from the live book. Keeping the new fields optional with fallbacks to
the existing integer DTE and to ScenarioParams means the picker/pasted paths and every current test
keep compiling and producing identical numbers (RESEARCH Pitfall 4 / Open Question 5).

Output: `dteExact()` in pair-calendars; `AnalyzerPosition` optional `frontDteExact`/`backDteExact`/
`frontRate`/`frontDivYield`/`backRate`/`backDivYield`; `/365.25` and per-leg carry resolution in
`calendarNetPrice`/`bookGreekAt`/`positionGreeksAt`; kernel-parity oracle tests.
</objective>
