<objective>
Build the two server-side foundations the parity-implied carry needs, both prerequisites for the
compute wiring in plan 34-04: (1) a pure `impliedDivYield` parity solver in the analytics domain, and
(2) the additive `mark` field on the GEX leg-obs read so the solver has raw call/put prices to work
from.

Purpose: CONTEXT item 2 replaces the flat DEFAULT_DIV guess with a per-expiry dividend yield implied
by put-call parity from our OWN stored chain. RESEARCH decomposes this into fixing `r` from FRED (plan
34-04) and solving the single remaining unknown `q` from parity — a well-conditioned one-unknown solve.
The solver is pure and testable in isolation with a hand-computed oracle; the raw marks it needs live
only server-side and are already read every GEX cycle via `readLegObsForGex`, which today projects
every field EXCEPT the mark.

Output: `impliedDivYield()` pure domain function with a parity round-trip oracle + fast-check property;
`LegObsForGex.mark` on the port, the Postgres SELECT, the in-memory twin, and the shared contract test.
</objective>
