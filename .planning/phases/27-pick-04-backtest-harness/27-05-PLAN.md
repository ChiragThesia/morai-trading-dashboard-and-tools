<objective>
Build the three replay paths — the heart of the harness — each assembling already-built readers (03) + already-built pure domain functions (02) and proving itself with a testcontainers integration test. Nothing here reimplements scoring or exit logic; each replay wires the untouched live functions.

- **[A] replayPickerCohort (BT-02):** the leakage oracle. For a stored `picker_snapshot`, replay the chain as-of-T through `selectCandidates` → `scoreCalendarCandidates` → `rankAndCapCandidates`, reusing the snapshot's FROZEN `gex`/`events`/status fields, and assert the replayed score reproduces the stored score EXACTLY (guarded by a ruleSet-drift check first). A mismatch is a hard failure printing the diverging id/rule.
- **[B] replayExitsForCalendar (BT-03):** walk each of the 13 closed calendars' full snapshot history through `evaluateExit` and compare the reproduced trajectory's direction (+ rough magnitude) against the summed `calendar_events.realizedPnl` fills-ledger oracle, haircut on entry AND exit.
- **[C] replayHypotheticalEntry (BT-04 input):** at each in-range cohort, score the FULL candidate universe (incl. gate-dropped strikes) with an optional per-rule weights override, then forward-walk each candidate through `evaluateExit` on a synthetic `MarketContext` assembled from the as-of-T chain via `computeLegPairMetrics`.

Purpose: BT-01 point-in-time replay through the same functions, BT-02 leakage oracle, BT-03 13-trade reproduction, BT-04's raw outcomes.
Output: the three replay functions + integration tests.
</objective>
