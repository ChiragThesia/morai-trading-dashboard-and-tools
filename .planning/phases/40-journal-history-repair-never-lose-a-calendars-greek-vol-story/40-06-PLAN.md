<objective>
Ship HIST-03's self-heal: a recurring worker job that repairs missing/gap slots for OPEN calendars over a bounded lookback (default 7 days), reusing the plan-05 rebuild engine. This is D-05's complement to OPS-01 — the live freshness gate keeps refusing to write stale marks as fresh, and self-heal separately repairs the slots that gate skipped, once usable data exists in `leg_observations`. Fill-only via the plan-04 heal-write, so a live row is never clobbered.

Wiring is Claude's discretion (CONTEXT.md delegates "chained vs cron"). Decision: a SEPARATE job (matching the dedicated-repair-job precedent — rebuild-journal, recompute-snapshot-pnl) on a sparse hourly cron, NO RTH gate. Rationale: healing PAST slots is not time-of-day sensitive; a sparse cadence bounds the scan cost; the fill-only heal makes every re-run a free no-op on already-healed rows. Chain-triggering after every 30-min cycle was considered and rejected as redundant scanning on the hot path.

The `self-heal-journal` Job Catalog row already landed in plan 01 (docs-before-code, D-10), so this plan's job code is docs-covered.

Purpose: Skips heal automatically going forward — no more permanent holes from a stale-leg cycle.
Output: `selfHealJournal` use-case + `self-heal-journal` job.
</objective>
